Coronavirus could force ISPs to abandon data caps forever
By Minyahel on March 07, 2020

                
                    

                        

                            
CONNECT WITH ME
   
                        

                        

                            
                                    
                                
                                
                            
                        

                    

                		
            

            

                
                    
    
                        




                        Pressure from the global pandemic has broadband companies loosening the arbitrary restrictions on the connections users pay for — and this may be the
                        beginning of the end for the data caps we’ve lived in fear of for decades. Here’s why.
                        The coronavirus threat and official policies of “social distancing” are leading millions to stay home, doing meetings via video chat and probably
                        watching Netflix and YouTube the rest of the time. That means a big uptick in bytes going through the tubes, both simultaneously and cumulatively.
                        ISPs, leery of repeating Verizon’s memorable gaffe of cutting off service during an emergency, are proposing a variety of user-friendly changes to 
                        their policies. Comcast is boosting the bandwidth of its low-income Internet Essentials customers to levels that actually qualify as broadband under 
                        FCC rules. AT&T is suspending data caps for all its customers until further notice. Verizon has added $500 million to its 5G rollout plans. Wait,
                        how does that help? Unclear, but the company “stands ready” for increases in traffic.

Amazon asks all employees to work from home, if they can
 
                    
By Minyahel on February 27, 2020

                    
                    
                        

                            




                            Amazon  issued guidance Thursday in response to the COVID-19 outbreak recommending that global employees who are able to work from home to do so 
                            through the end of March.
                            “We continue to work closely with public and private medical experts to ensure we are taking the right precautions as the situation continues to
                            evolve,” an Amazon spokesperson said in an email statement. “As a result, we are now recommending that all of our employees globally who are able
